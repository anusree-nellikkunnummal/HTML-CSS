Alappuzha

It's best known for houseboat cruises along the rustic Kerala backwaters, a network of tranquil canals and lagoons. Alappuzha Beach is the site of the 19th-century Alappuzha Lighthouse. Eranakulam

Sprawling, residential Ernakulam is known for Marine Drive, a busy waterfront promenade where boats offer backwater cruises. The Kerala Folklore and Hill Palace museums explore local heritage through art and antiquities. Shops along Broadway and on MG Road sell fabrics, crafts, and spices, while modern Lulu Mall also has a cinema and an ice rink. Simple eateries serve Keralan specialties and South Indian seafood.

Kannur

Kannur is a coastal city in the south Indian state of Kerala. It was once an ancient trading port. Enduring monuments such as 16th-century St. Angelo Fort, once occupied by European colonial forces, show the city’s significant role in the spice trade. Housed in a former palace, the Arakkal Museum highlights Kerala's one Muslim royal family. The palm-fringed sands of Payyambalam Beach run along Kannur’s western shore. Kollam

Kollam is a city in the state of Kerala, on India's Malabar Coast. It’s known as a trade hub and for its beaches, like lively Kollam and secluded Thirumullavaram. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Police Museum has artifacts tracing the history of the police force. Nearby, Ashtamudi Lake is a gateway to the Kerala backwaters, a network of waterways rich with vegetation. The striped 1902 Tangasseri Lighthouse has ocean views..

Wayanad

The mountains of the Western Ghats are the green borders of Kerala and nestled amidst this green paradise is our lovely Wayanad. Emerald green plantations, verdant forests, exotic wildlife, lofty ridges, rugged terrains and deep valleys, it will give you numerous reasons to choose why it is a must visit tourist destination in God’s own country – Kerala. Every traveler finds this enchanting destination worth his/her time. Be it an adventurer who would love to discover trekking trails, water sports and wildlife safaris, couples who would love to discover picture perfect landscapes to cherish some lovely moments of togetherness and a nature lover who would love to become one with nature. One must visit this lovely tourist destination to escape the chaos of the world. Wayanad What’s in the Name Wayanad has a very interesting tale attached to its name. Wayanad – Vayal Nadu means rice plantation. It is said that Wayanad always remains fertile and green and it is very popular for its paddy fields. Hence it is referred to as Vayal Nadu and in today’s times it is changed to Wayanad.
Wayanad is blessed with wildlife sanctuaries, lush tea estates and dense forests. Its eastern border is decked with Nilgiris in Tamil Nadu, Western border is blessed with beautiful hill stations like Coorg. It has plenty of mesmerizing places for nature tourism.Peace, serenity and tranquility is what we seek amidst nature. Wayanad is not connected through railways. It is less commercialized in comparison to other cities in Kerala, Hence, It is one of the best jewels for nature tourism in Kerala.Want to see Kerala’s own pine forests? Wayanad is the place where in Chandanathode at Mananthavady you will be seduced by the greenery of pine forests.For evergreen landscapes and blissful weather Wayanad is often referred to as Ooty of Kerala.If you want to see the only dam which is made up of earth materials then visit Banasura Sagar Dam in Wayanad. It is a beautiful nature heaven surrounded by beautiful Banasura hills in the background.
